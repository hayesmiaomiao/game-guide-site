## Detailed Guide

### Why This Topic Matters

Rune Arc Farming affects how smoothly a character progresses through the Lands Between. Some items improve raw power, some unlock flexibility, and some make difficult areas less punishing. The important thing is to connect each pickup to a practical decision. Do not collect blindly without knowing whether the reward supports your build, your next boss, or your completion goal. ### Early Route Priorities

In the early game, prioritize low-risk pickups that improve survivability or unlock future options. Explore Limgrave, the Weeping Peninsula, and Liurnia with map fragments active so you can identify churches, ruins, towers, caves, mines, and roads. If the topic depends on merchants or bell bearings, turn items in at Roundtable Hold as soon as possible. If it depends on a tower puzzle or sealed door, mark it and return when the requirement is clear. Early efficiency is not about grabbing everything; it is about grabbing what makes the next five hours smoother.

### Mid and Late Game Cleanup

By Altus, Leyndell, Mountaintops, and optional underground regions, cleanup becomes more important. Many high-value items are near bosses, legacy dungeon branches, hidden elevators, or quest-related paths. Before entering the final stretch, review your build and ask what is still missing: final upgrades, spell slots, flask improvements, crafting options, or completion items. This is the best time to clear marked locations because your character is strong enough to survive dangerous zones and your map is detailed enough to route efficiently.

### How to Avoid Wasted Time

Use map markers aggressively. Mark locked doors, suspicious towers, inaccessible ledges, and NPC hints. When a guide mentions a location you have not revealed, collect that region's map fragment first. For rare upgrade items or one-time choices, wait until you are confident about the weapon or reward. If a resource can be duplicated, purchased later, or farmed safely, it is less risky. If it is limited, spend it only after testing the tool in real fights.
