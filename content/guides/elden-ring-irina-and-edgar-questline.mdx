## Quick Answer

The best way to complete the Irina and Edgar Questline in Elden Ring is to treat it as a regional chain instead of a single objective marker. Start by meeting Irina and Edgar at the earliest location, exhaust dialogue until lines repeat, then revisit the character after major bosses, region unlocks, or Roundtable Hold changes. This route focuses on Castle Morne letter delivery, Leonine Misbegotten, and Edgar's invasion. If a step seems stalled, check whether you skipped an earlier conversation, missed a key item, or advanced another questline that moves the NPC forward.

## Detailed Guide

### Where to Start the Questline

Begin by finding the NPC in the earliest part of the route: Weeping Peninsula and Revenger's Shack. Speak until dialogue repeats. This matters because many Elden Ring questlines only advance after all available lines have been heard. If the NPC offers an item, request, gesture, shop, or warning, treat that as a signal that the route has been activated. Resting at a Site of Grace, reloading the area, or returning after a nearby boss can update the next state.

Do not attack NPCs or assume a strange character is disposable. Elden Ring often uses suspicious, hostile, or morally complicated NPCs for valuable rewards. If you are unsure whether a choice is permanent, delay it until you know what the reward and story consequences are.

### Main Route Order

Follow the quest in regional passes. Clear the immediate conversation, complete the nearby request, then check Roundtable Hold or the next region tied to the NPC. For Irina and Edgar Questline, the important route is Weeping Peninsula and Revenger's Shack. Keep a manual note when the NPC mentions a place, person, item, or problem. Those lines are often the closest thing Elden Ring gives to a quest marker.

After major bosses, revisit the NPC. Boss kills can move characters to new locations, unlock new dialogue, or close an earlier branch. If the NPC is gone, check the next likely story location before assuming the quest failed. Many characters move forward silently after you complete a required milestone.

### Choices, Rewards, and Missable Steps

The Irina and Edgar Questline can involve choices that affect rewards, dialogue, or ending availability. Before committing to a hostile action, potion delivery, invasion sign, or final interaction, consider whether the route is tied to another NPC. Some questlines overlap, and one character's progress can change another character's outcome. If your goal is completion, prioritize collecting unique items, spells, gestures, armor, bell bearings, or mending rune rewards before pushing into irreversible late-game story changes.

If a reward does not appear, return to previous locations and check whether an item remains on the ground, a summon sign is active, or a dialogue option has been missed. Elden Ring often places quest rewards at the NPC's last location rather than handing them directly to the player.

### When to Finish the Questline

Finish the route before the final stretch if the quest affects endings, legendary items, or late-game NPC states. A good habit is to perform a quest cleanup pass after reaching Altus, again after Leyndell, and again before the final boss. For Irina and Edgar Questline, make sure the main reward has been collected and any related NPCs have been checked. This prevents the most frustrating situation: knowing the route exists only after the world state has moved past it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I miss the Irina and Edgar Questline?

Yes, parts of the quest can be missed or moved forward by boss kills, region progression, or late-game world changes. Regular dialogue checks reduce that risk.

### What should I do if Irina and Edgar disappears?

Check the next route location, Roundtable Hold, and the NPC's previous location. Many characters move after major milestones without a direct marker.

### Does this quest affect an ending?

Some NPC quests affect endings while others mainly reward items, spells, armor, or lore. If an ending item or mending rune appears, keep it safe until the final choice.

### Should I finish this quest before the final boss?

Yes. Quest cleanup before the final boss is the safest approach, especially for routes tied to endings, legendary rewards, or late-game areas.

### Is this quest worth doing on a first playthrough?

Yes. Even if you do not chase every branch, the route gives context, rewards, and useful reasons to explore areas you might otherwise skip.
