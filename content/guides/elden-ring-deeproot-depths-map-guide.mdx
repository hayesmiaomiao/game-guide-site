## Detailed Guide

### First Route Through the Area

Start by finding the safest Site of Grace and reading the map layout before deep exploration. Roads, bridges, cliffs, towers, churches, ruins, and caves usually tell you where the area wants you to go. In Deeproot Depths, prioritize Fia route, coffin access, roots navigation, and hidden boss progression. Do not try to clear every enemy on the first pass. Ride or move cautiously, unlock graces, collect obvious map information, and mark places that need a key, ladder, elevator, or later quest step.

### Key Landmarks and Upgrade Stops

Every major region has a few landmarks that matter more than random enemy camps. Churches often improve flask strength, mines improve weapon upgrades, towers may unlock memory stones or puzzle rewards, and legacy dungeon branches often hide talismans or shortcuts. When exploring Deeproot Depths, connect each landmark to a purpose: upgrades, boss preparation, quest progress, farming, or completion. If a location does not help your current goal, mark it and move on rather than forcing it immediately.

### Bosses, Quests, and Hidden Paths

Map guides are strongest when they connect bosses and quests together. Check whether Deeproot Depths contains an NPC step, optional boss, underground connection, sealed route, or hidden shortcut. Many Elden Ring questlines depend on returning to areas after another boss or region has been completed. If an NPC mentions a place that sounds vague, search the map for matching landmarks and keep notes. Hidden paths are often suggested by item placement, suspicious architecture, or unreachable loot visible from another angle.

### Cleanup Route

After the first clear, do a cleanup pass. Start from each unlocked Site of Grace and look for unopened doors, unexplored cliff edges, elevators you did not ride, rooftops you skipped, and map markers you placed earlier. Cleanup is also the time to grab items that were too dangerous during the first pass. If the area connects to late-game quests or endings, finish those steps before pushing the final story milestones.

## Tips

- Reveal the map and unlock Sites of Grace before full cleanup.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When should I explore Deeproot Depths?

Explore it when your weapon upgrades, flask strength, and Vigor feel appropriate for the enemies. If damage feels too low, return after nearby upgrades.

### What should I prioritize first?

Prioritize Sites of Grace, map information, churches, mines, major quest landmarks, and shortcuts before optional enemy camps.

### Are there hidden paths in Deeproot Depths?

Most major Elden Ring areas contain hidden paths, vertical routes, locked doors, or quest-related returns. Use markers when something looks suspicious.

### Should I fight every boss immediately?

No. Optional bosses can wait until your build is ready. Unlock the route first, then choose fights based on rewards and difficulty.

### How do I finish cleanup efficiently?

Start from each Site of Grace, check every branch, clear map markers, and revisit locked or suspicious places after quests and boss milestones.
