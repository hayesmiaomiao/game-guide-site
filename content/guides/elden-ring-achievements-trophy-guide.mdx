## Quick Answer

Elden Ring achievements and trophies are easiest when planned around three pillars: major bosses, legendary collectibles, and endings. Most boss achievements come naturally from exploration and progression, but legendary armaments, spells, talismans, and ashes require deliberate cleanup. Ending trophies require quest planning, especially Ranni and Frenzied Flame routes. For a smooth completion run, track legendary items as you enter each region, finish major NPC questlines before the final boss, and avoid locking yourself into an ending before you understand your options.

## Introduction

A full Elden Ring completion run is less about perfect mechanical skill and more about route discipline. The game is huge, and achievement hunters can lose hours backtracking for a missed legendary item or quest state. This guide gives you a practical framework for reaching completion without turning every session into spreadsheet work.

## Detailed Guide

### Achievement Categories

Most achievements fall into boss kills, endings, legendary collections, and major progression milestones. Boss achievements reward defeating important shardbearers and optional threats. Collection achievements require finding every item in a specific legendary category. Ending achievements require unlocking and choosing specific outcomes. Understanding these categories helps you route efficiently: bosses test power, collections test exploration, and endings test quest awareness.

### Endings and Save Planning

Ending achievements are the most planning-heavy part. Age of Stars requires Ranni's questline, while Frenzied Flame requires a deliberate late-game path. Other ending variants may depend on mending runes and NPC questlines. If you are playing on a platform where save backups are possible, some players use them near the ending choice, but the cleanest organic route is to plan multiple playthroughs or keep options open until the final decision.

### Legendary Collectibles

Legendary armaments, spells, talismans, and Spirit Ashes are easy to miss because they are scattered across optional areas, boss rewards, quest routes, and hidden locations. Track each category separately. When entering a new region, check whether any legendary item belongs there before moving on. Some items can become awkward to obtain after story changes, so do collection cleanup before the final stretch.

### Boss and Cleanup Route

Achievement bosses should be approached after your weapon and flasks are properly upgraded. Optional bosses like Malenia demand preparation, while many remembrance bosses are easier when you return later. Use the map to mark unfinished caves, towers, and legacy dungeon branches. A systematic cleanup pass before New Game Plus saves more time than random wandering after the credits.

## Tips

- Track endings separately from collectibles.
- Mark legendary items by region.
- Do late-game cleanup before the final boss.
- Upgrade weapons and flasks before optional achievement bosses.

## Common Mistakes

- Leaving legendary collectibles for memory-based cleanup.
- Locking into Frenzied Flame without planning.
- Ignoring NPC questlines tied to endings.
- Starting New Game Plus before checking missing trophies.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Elden Ring hard to platinum?

It is time-consuming but manageable with planning, especially for endings and legendary collectibles.

### Are any achievements missable?

Some rewards and ending paths can become difficult or impossible depending on progression, so plan major questlines early.

### Do I need to beat Malenia for achievements?

Yes, Malenia is tied to completion goals and is one of the hardest optional challenges.

### Can one playthrough unlock every ending achievement?

Normally you choose one ending per completion, though platform-specific save handling can change how players approach this.

### What should I track first?

Track legendary collectibles and ending questlines first because they are easiest to overlook.

Achievements and Trophy gives players a clearer route through one of Elden Ring's most easily missed systems.
