## Quick Answer

Smithing Stones are the backbone of weapon progression in Elden Ring. The fastest way to keep damage competitive is to explore mines marked by dark cave icons on the map, collect stones from tunnels, and unlock bell bearings that let the Twin Maiden Husks sell upgrade tiers at Roundtable Hold. Prioritize one main weapon first, avoid spreading stones across every new drop, and check mines in Limgrave, Liurnia, Caelid, Altus, and later regions as your upgrade needs rise.

## Introduction

Many Elden Ring difficulty spikes are really upgrade problems. A player with a focused +6 or +12 weapon often has a smoother time than a higher-level character using several underdeveloped weapons. This guide explains where to look for Smithing Stones, how bell bearings simplify farming, and how to spend materials without trapping your build.

## Detailed Guide

### How Smithing Stone Progression Works

Regular Smithing Stones upgrade standard weapons in numbered tiers, while Somber Smithing Stones upgrade unique weapons separately. This guide focuses on regular Smithing Stones, but the planning principle is the same: concentrate materials into a weapon you actually use. Early stones are common enough to test a few options, but mid-tier stones can bottleneck if you upgrade every weapon that looks interesting. Before spending, decide whether the weapon fits your stats, moveset preference, and near-term boss plan.

### Best Early Places to Find Stones

Limgrave Tunnels is one of the first reliable sources and teaches the visual language of mines: miners, stone walls, and glowing pickups along the route. The Weeping Peninsula and Liurnia offer more upgrade materials as you expand. Mines are usually marked on the map with small cave-like symbols once the map fragment is found. If your damage feels low, check the map for unexplored tunnels before grinding random enemies. Exploring mines also gives runes and sometimes weapon drops, making them efficient progression stops.

### Bell Bearings and Buying Stones

Smithing-Stone Miner's Bell Bearings are the long-term solution. When given to the Twin Maiden Husks at Roundtable Hold, they unlock purchase options for specific stone tiers. This turns upgrades from a scavenger hunt into a rune cost. Bell bearings are spread through mines and later areas, so they naturally track your progression. Once you unlock a tier, use purchased stones to test secondary weapons, but still reserve rare higher-tier materials until you know the weapon deserves them.

### Upgrade Priority

Upgrade your main weapon before chasing minor stat gains. Damage scaling improves with stats, but raw weapon level is often the larger early boost. If you are unsure between two weapons, raise both only to a low shared tier, test movesets in real fights, then commit. Shields, bows, and backup weapons should wait unless they are central to your strategy. A clean upgrade plan makes bosses shorter, safer, and less dependent on perfect play.

## Tips

- Look for mine icons after collecting each region map fragment.
- Upgrade one main weapon before experimenting heavily.
- Turn in bell bearings immediately at Roundtable Hold.
- Separate regular and Somber upgrade plans so materials are not confused.

## Common Mistakes

- Upgrading five weapons equally and leaving all of them weak.
- Ignoring mines while trying to solve damage with levels only.
- Using rare late-tier stones before testing the moveset.
- Forgetting that purchased stones still require enough runes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Where should I farm early Smithing Stones?

Start with Limgrave Tunnels and other regional mines, then move into Liurnia and later areas as tier requirements increase.

### Can I buy Smithing Stones?

Yes, after finding the correct Smithing-Stone Miner's Bell Bearings and giving them to the Twin Maiden Husks.

### Should I upgrade multiple weapons?

Test a few at low levels, but focus higher-tier stones on one main weapon until your build is stable.

### Are Smithing Stones and Somber Smithing Stones the same?

No. Regular weapons use Smithing Stones, while unique weapons use Somber Smithing Stones.

### Why is my damage low?

Your weapon may be under-upgraded. Check mines and bell bearings before assuming your character level is the problem.
