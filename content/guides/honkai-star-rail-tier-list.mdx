## Tier List Criteria

This tier list is designed for new accounts. It values characters that work in many teams, perform well before perfect relics, and help players clear story and farming content consistently.

The ranking criteria are:

- Account flexibility
- Low-investment performance
- Story and farming speed
- Survival support
- Long-term team value

## Detailed Guide

A useful Honkai Star Rail tier list for new accounts should answer a different question than an endgame damage chart. New players are not only asking which character can produce the biggest number under ideal relic conditions. They need to know which characters make story progress smoother, which units stay valuable across many teams, and which investments are least likely to become wasted materials. That is why this guide gives more weight to reliability, role coverage, and low-investment performance than to narrow showcase potential.

The first priority for most accounts is survival. A strong sustain character keeps underbuilt teams alive while you are still learning enemy weaknesses, break timing, and skill point flow. Early relics are often messy, traces are incomplete, and Light Cone choices may be limited, so a comfortable sustain can do more for progression than another damage dealer. If you already have a reliable healer or shielder, the next priority is usually a flexible support who can improve several different carries instead of only one specialized team.

Damage still matters, but early damage should be judged by consistency. A good new-account damage dealer clears common waves, handles story bosses without perfect relics, and does not require rare teammates to function. Characters that demand strict speed tuning, high Eidolons, or very specific premium supports can still be excellent later, but they are less beginner-friendly. When you compare two damage options, choose the one that works with your current roster and your available resources, not the one that performs best in a fully optimized simulation.

Team flexibility is another major ranking factor. A character who fits many team shells helps you adapt as banners change and your account grows. Universal supports, efficient breakers, and sustain units with broad utility usually age well because they solve problems across multiple teams. Narrow units can be worth building when you love their playstyle or already own the required partners, but they should not consume all early resources before your account has a stable core.

For practical progression, build one complete team before spreading materials across several half-built options. Level your main damage dealer, keep your sustain current, and invest in supports that improve the whole team's output or safety. Do not chase perfect relic substats too early. Main stats, set basics, traces, and Light Cone levels provide more reliable gains while your Trailblaze Level is still rising.

Finally, treat every tier list as a planning tool rather than a permanent rule. Honkai Star Rail changes as new enemies, characters, Light Cones, and mechanics arrive. A character ranked lower today can become more useful when a new support or enemy weakness pattern appears. Use this list to avoid obvious early mistakes, then adjust based on your roster, your favorite playstyle, and the current patch environment.

## Tips

- Build around roles before rarity. A balanced new account usually needs sustain, support, and one reliable damage dealer more than another narrow DPS option.
- Do not judge a character only by showcase damage. Early accounts often lack relic quality, trace levels, and team pieces that make premium rotations work.
- Keep one flexible breaker leveled enough for story bosses. Element coverage can save more time than chasing perfect tier placement.
- Save limited resources until you know whether a character improves multiple teams or only one specific setup.

## Common Mistakes

### Ranking by Rarity Alone

Five-star characters can be excellent, but rarity does not automatically make a unit the best choice for a new account. A lower-rarity sustain, breaker, or support can outperform a premium damage dealer if the account lacks the team pieces needed to support that damage.

### Overinvesting in Too Many Damage Dealers

New players often level several attackers at once and then run out of credits, trace materials, and relic experience. Pick one main carry for story progress, then build supports that make that carry more consistent.

### Ignoring Team Comfort

A fragile high-damage team may look strong on paper but perform badly during long story fights. If your account dies often, a better sustain or defensive support is usually a bigger upgrade than another damage increase.

### Pulling Without a Role Plan

Before spending limited currency, ask what problem the banner character solves. Pulling for favorites is fine, but from an account-growth perspective, each pull should improve a real weakness.
