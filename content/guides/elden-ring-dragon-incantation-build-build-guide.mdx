## Detailed Guide

### What This Setup Is Best For

The Dragon Incantation Build is strongest when its main advantage is used consistently. Some setups win through burst damage, some through stance pressure, some through status buildup, and some through safety or sustain. Do not judge it only by a single boss clip. Ask whether it helps with exploration, repeated enemies, difficult bosses, and your preferred pace of play. If the setup requires perfect spacing or rare gear before it works, treat the early version as a stepping stone rather than the final goal.

### Stats and Upgrade Priority

Meet minimum requirements first, then invest in the scaling stats that improve your main damage source. For most builds, Vigor remains important because a dead character deals no damage. Endurance matters if the setup uses heavy weapons, shields, repeated attacks, or armor weight. Mind matters for weapon skills and spells, but only if FP limits are actually stopping your route. Upgrade level should be your first damage check. A properly upgraded weapon often outperforms a poorly upgraded weapon with better-looking stat scaling.

### Early, Mid, and Late Game Route

In the early game, use the simplest version of the setup with accessible gear and conservative talismans. The goal is to make the playstyle function without rare items. In mid game, add the pieces that improve consistency: better scaling, stronger ashes of war, flask upgrades, and talismans that match your attack pattern. In late game, optimize around the fights that actually give you trouble. A setup that destroys normal enemies may need defensive swaps, status support, or ranged options for bosses with short punish windows.

### Boss and Exploration Adjustments

For exploration, value sustain, crowd control, and low-risk attacks. For bosses, value repeatable punish windows. If the Dragon Incantation Build has a long animation, use it after large recoveries rather than forcing it after every dodge. If it relies on status, remember that some enemies resist or punish close-range pressure. Carry a backup plan that preserves the same stat investment, such as a second weapon, faster skill, shield option, or ranged spell.

## Tips

- Upgrade the main weapon or casting tool before spreading materials.
- Keep enough Vigor and stamina for mistakes and recovery.
- Choose talismans that support your real punish window.
- Test the setup against both normal enemies and bosses.
- Carry one backup option for resistant enemies.

## Common Mistakes

- Copying an endgame setup before the required stats are available.
- Ignoring weapon level and blaming the build for low damage.
- Stacking damage bonuses while dying in one combo.
- Using long animations in tiny punish windows.
- Refusing to swap talismans for specific bosses.
