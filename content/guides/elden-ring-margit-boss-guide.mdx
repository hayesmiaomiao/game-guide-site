## Quick Answer

Margit is the first major skill check in Elden Ring, but he becomes manageable when you stop chasing every opening and fight around his delayed attacks. Reach at least level 20, upgrade one main weapon, bring Spirit Ashes or Sorcerer Rogier if you want help, and punish only after clear recovery animations. The safest plan is to stay just outside his cane swings, roll late through delayed strikes, and use jump attacks or guard counters after his longer combos. Margit rewards patience more than raw damage.

## Introduction

Margit, the Fell Omen guards the approach to Stormveil Castle and teaches the most important lesson in Elden Ring: bosses are designed to punish panic rolls. His attacks hesitate, restart, and chain into holy dagger follow-ups that catch players who dodge on rhythm instead of animation. This guide gives you a practical route for beating Margit without overleveling the fight into a blur.

## Detailed Guide

### Recommended Level and Preparation

Aim for level 20 to 30 with a weapon upgraded to at least +3 if it uses regular Smithing Stones. Vigor matters more than one or two extra damage levels, because surviving a delayed hit gives you another chance to learn the pattern. If you started as a melee class, use a shield only when you understand its limits; Margit can break stamina quickly if you block every hit. Ranged and magic characters should still carry a melee backup because Margit closes distance aggressively. Before entering, spend runes, refill flasks, and decide whether this attempt is for learning or winning. A learning attempt should focus on recognizing the cane windups and golden dagger follow-ups rather than burning every flask early.

### Safe Openings

The safest punish comes after Margit leaps into the air and slams down, after his long overhead cane swing, and after certain two-hit cane strings where he visibly resets. Do not attack after every small dagger swipe. Those quick holy blades are bait, especially when you stand close to his front hand. Jump attacks are strong because they build stance damage, but use one jump attack and back away instead of trying to force a full combo. If you use a heavy weapon, charge attacks are possible only after his largest recovery windows. Fast weapons can land two light attacks, but greed is the main reason players lose this fight.

### How to Handle Phase Two

At roughly half health Margit adds the glowing hammer and longer holy weapon chains. The hammer leap is dangerous but also one of your best openings if you roll through the impact and punish from the side. When he draws the hammer while standing still, create space and let the sequence finish. His phase two combos feel chaotic because he changes tempo mid-string. Watch the shoulders and weapon hand rather than his feet. If you are using summons, let them draw attention during phase two and use the time to heal, cast, or land stance-building hits from the flank.

### Best Tools for the Fight

Spirit Ashes make the fight much easier by splitting Margit's attention. Lone Wolf Ashes can create useful pressure early, while tankier ashes give slower builds time to recover. Sorcerer Rogier is also available as an NPC summon outside the fog gate and is helpful for new players. Margit's Shackle, purchased from Patches after sparing him, can pin Margit briefly during the first phase. It is not required, but it gives melee players safe damage and a calmer start. Use it early rather than saving it for a perfect moment that never comes.

## Tips

- Roll later than your instinct tells you; many cane attacks are intentionally delayed.
- Use one punish per opening until you can read the full combo.
- Spend runes before attempts so the fight feels less stressful.
- If phase two overwhelms you, use summons to create healing windows.

## Common Mistakes

- Rolling as soon as Margit raises his weapon.
- Attacking after quick dagger swipes.
- Entering with an unupgraded weapon and very low Vigor.
- Healing directly in front of him instead of creating distance first.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What level should I be for Margit?

Most players should fight Margit around level 20 to 30 with a modestly upgraded weapon and enough Vigor to survive a mistake.

### Is Margit optional?

Margit blocks the main route into Stormveil Castle, but you can explore Limgrave and the Weeping Peninsula first to gain levels and upgrades.

### Should I summon Rogier for Margit?

Yes, if you want a smoother first clear. Rogier can distract Margit and create safer healing or damage windows.

### Does Margit's Shackle still work in phase two?

Margit's Shackle is mainly useful early in the fight and should be used before relying on phase two pressure.

### What is the biggest Margit mistake?

The biggest mistake is panic rolling. Margit delays attacks specifically to catch early dodges.
