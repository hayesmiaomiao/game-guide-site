## Quick Start Priorities

Your first goal is not to clear every enemy. It is to unlock Sites of Grace, collect map fragments, upgrade your main weapon, and build enough Flask charges to survive mistakes.

Focus on these early priorities:

- Unlock Church of Elleh and Gatefront Ruins.
- Get Torrent as soon as Melina appears.
- Pick one main weapon and upgrade it instead of spreading materials.
- Put early levels into Vigor before chasing damage stats.
- Use guard counters if you start with a shield.

## First 10 Hours Route

Start in Limgrave and move carefully through low-risk camps. Gatefront Ruins is a strong practice area because enemies are spaced out and the Site of Grace is close.

After you unlock Torrent, ride south toward the Weeping Peninsula. This area is more forgiving than Stormveil Castle and gives you extra upgrade materials, Sacred Tears, and Golden Seeds.

## Early Stat Advice

For most beginners, Vigor should reach 20 before damage stats become a priority. Surviving one extra hit matters more than adding a few points of weapon scaling.

If you are unsure what to level:

1. Raise Vigor.
2. Meet minimum weapon requirements.
3. Add Endurance if your gear load is too heavy.
4. Improve Strength, Dexterity, Intelligence, Faith, or Arcane only after your build direction is clear.

## Boss Preparation

Before fighting Margit, aim for a +3 weapon, extra Flask charges, and a Spirit Ash summon you understand. Margit punishes panic rolls, so watch the delayed attacks and roll after the swing begins.

## Common Beginner Mistakes

Do not sell unique weapons, ignore crafting recipes, or assume every visible boss must be defeated immediately. Elden Ring rewards leaving hard fights, finding power elsewhere, and coming back prepared.

## Detailed Guide

The first ten hours of Elden Ring should be about building a stable character, not proving you can beat every enemy immediately. New players often lose time because they walk straight from the tutorial into the most intimidating path and assume that is the intended route. Elden Ring is much more flexible. If a boss or area feels unreasonable, the game usually expects you to explore, upgrade, and return with better tools.

Start by learning Limgrave's safe loop. Church of Elleh, Gatefront Ruins, nearby caves, and the roads around the starting area teach the basics without demanding perfect play. Unlock Sites of Grace whenever you see them, collect the first map fragment, and rest at enough Grace points to meet Melina and receive Torrent. Torrent changes the early game because you can scout, escape, and reach upgrade materials much faster.

Gatefront Ruins is a useful practice zone because it has guards, dogs, shields, patrols, and a nearby reset point. Do not only farm it for runes. Use it to practice pulling one enemy at a time, backstabbing, guard counters, jump attacks, and retreating when a fight becomes messy. These fundamentals matter throughout the whole game. A player who learns spacing and stamina management early will have an easier time than a player who only rushes levels.

After Torrent, move south toward Weeping Peninsula instead of forcing Stormveil immediately. This route gives you more Flask upgrades, more Smithing Stones, and easier combat practice. The extra Sacred Tears and Golden Seeds make a huge difference for beginners because they forgive mistakes during longer fights. If you find a weapon you enjoy, upgrade it steadily rather than switching every few minutes. A familiar +3 weapon is often stronger than a flashy unupgraded weapon with awkward requirements.

Stat planning should stay simple. Vigor is the safest early investment for almost every build because it gives you more chances to learn. Meet the minimum requirements for your chosen weapon, keep your equip load below heavy roll, and delay specialized damage scaling until you know what kind of build you want. Strength, Dexterity, Intelligence, Faith, and Arcane all become useful later, but early survival and weapon upgrades usually matter more.

When you prepare for Margit, aim for comfort rather than pride. A better Flask, a leveled weapon, Spirit Ashes, and some practice with delayed attacks are all legitimate preparation. If Margit still feels too hard, leave again. Explore mines, clear smaller dungeons, collect materials, and return when your character and your timing are both better. That loop is the heart of Elden Ring progression.

## Tips

- Treat the first hours as preparation. Unlock travel points, Flask upgrades, map pieces, and Torrent before judging whether an area is too hard.
- Spend early Smithing Stones on one weapon you actually use instead of upgrading every interesting drop.
- Level Vigor before chasing damage scaling. Surviving one more hit helps new players learn boss timing faster.
- Leave optional bosses alone when they stall progress. Elden Ring is designed around returning stronger later.

## Common Mistakes

### Forcing Stormveil Too Early

Margit and Stormveil are meant to teach you that leaving is allowed. If the fight feels impossible, explore Limgrave and Weeping Peninsula first instead of repeating the same underpowered attempt.

### Ignoring Weapon Upgrades

A few weapon upgrades usually matter more than several early damage-stat levels. If your hits feel weak, check your Smithing Stone path before assuming your build is wrong.

### Rolling Too Early

Many Elden Ring bosses delay attacks. New players often roll as soon as the weapon lifts, then get hit by the actual swing. Watch the release, not the windup.

### Hoarding Every Consumable

Consumables are part of the toolkit. Fire pots, throwing knives, grease, and resistance items can turn rough early fights into manageable ones.

This first 10 hours route gives you more healing, better damage, and enough confidence to learn fights without losing hours to avoidable mistakes.
